The US Case Study

The Phillips Curve posits a stable, inverse relationship between inflation and unemployment. However, a visual inspection of the US long-term data reveals an inconsistent and noisy signal.

The Phillips Curve is Unreliable

Weak in the US, inverted in the CEE. It is a poor guide for modern policy making.

Growth is Key

Fostering GDP growth is the most robust strategy for reducing unemployment across systems.

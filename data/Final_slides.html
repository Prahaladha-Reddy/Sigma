The US Case:
Decoupled

In the US, the long-term data demonstrates a complete breakdown of the traditional Phillips Curve trade-off.

KEY FINDING

Regime-dependent instability. The signal is effectively lost in the noise.

Disaggregation by decade reveals scattered plots with near-zero Pearson correlation coefficients.

FIGURE 1.0: US unemployment vs. inflation, colored by decade. The scattered plot demonstrates the absence of a persistent inverse relationship, confirming the variables are effectively decoupled.

IMPULSE RESPONSE: Positive response of unemployment to inflation shock. Inflation explains >23% of unemployment variance over a 10-period horizon.

Anomaly Detected

The CEE Case:
Inverted & Stagflationary

Contrary to standard theory, the Central & Eastern European region exhibits a dangerous inversion of the curve.

Panel VAR Analysis

Inflationary shocks lead to a statistically significant increase in unemployment. Rising prices actively weaken the labor market.

Strategic Implications

Moving beyond the theoretical trade-off

Signal Failure

The Phillips Curve is an unreliable policy guide. The relationship is nonexistent in the US and dangerously inverted in the CEE region.

Status: Broken Model

Context is Determinant

Structural factors in transitioning economies create supply-side vulnerabilities. This produces stagflationary outcomes where inflation harms employment.

Driver: Structure

The Unifying Principle

Across all regions, sustainable GDP growth is the only robust driver of lower unemployment.

Focus: Growth
Recommendation: Shift to dual focus on Price Stability + Pro-Growth Structural Reforms
